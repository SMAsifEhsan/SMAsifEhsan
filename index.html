About

Biography

Development economist with expertise in applied econometrics, environmental policy, and labor economics.

Dr. S. M. Asif Ehsan is an Associate Professor and the Chairperson of the Department of Economics at North South University, Bangladesh. His primary research interests lie in applied econometrics, environmental and resource economics, and development economics.

As an empirical researcher, Dr. Ehsan engages in core economic studies and has contributed to several interdisciplinary research projects supported by organizations such as the International Development Research Centre (IDRC, Canada), the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), UNDP, Commonwealth Secretariat, Concern Worldwide, and the Bangladesh Planning Commission. His research addresses a wide range of socio-economic and environmental issues, and he has published in prominent journals including World Development Perspectives, Energy Research & Social Science, Economic Papers, and Sustainable Development. His full publication record is available on Google Scholar and Web of Science.

He serves as Co-Editor of the journal Development Letters and as a Reviewer for journals including Economic Papers and Poverty & Public Policy. At North South University, Dr. Ehsan teaches graduate courses, covering topics such as panel data analysis, time series forecasting, and game theory.

Dr. Ehsan earned his Ph.D. in Economics from Virginia Tech, USA, where his dissertation explored the impacts of contract duration on environmental outcomes in the hydraulic fracturing industry, under the supervision of Dr. Nicolaus Tideman. Prior to his Ph.D., he completed his bachelor's and master's degrees in Economics from the University of Dhaka, graduating with first-class honors in both.

Research

Research Interests

Spanning core economic studies and interdisciplinary projects addressing socio-economic and environmental challenges in developing countries.

📊

Development Economics

Multidimensional poverty analysis, ultra-poor identification frameworks, social protection programs, tea garden worker welfare, and sustainable development in Bangladesh's urban slums and rural areas.

📈

Applied Econometrics

Panel data analysis, time series forecasting, stochastic frontier analysis, generalized ordered logit models, and probit binary response regression applied to real-world socio-economic problems.

🌿

Environmental Economics

Climate vulnerability assessment, disaster-induced agricultural productivity, nature-based solutions for urban resilience, solar energy sustainability, and energy poverty in disaster-prone regions.

👥

Labor Economics

Female labor force participation under adverse shocks, household labor allocation decisions in rice farming, economic opportunities in the informal sector, and parenting time dynamics.
